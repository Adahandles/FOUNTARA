Restoration & Preservation Grant Proposal Outline

This outline summarises the proposed multi‑phase restoration and preservation effort for the Orange Springs property. Each phase focuses on a different aspect of acquisition, rehabilitation, and long‑term stewardship.

  1. Phase 1: Acquisition & Stabilisation (0‑6 months) – Secure ownership of the spring and surrounding land, fence and stabilise the site, and perform initial clean‑up and hazard mitigation. Funding sources: Fountara investment and matching grants.
  2. Phase 2: Environmental Restoration (6‑18 months) – Remove invasive vegetation, replant native species, restore the spring run and riparian habitats, and monitor water quality. Funding sources: conservation grants, volunteer labour, partnerships with environmental groups.
  3. Phase 3: Historical Structure Rehabilitation (12‑24 months) – Restore the historic Townsend House and any remaining structures, ensuring structural integrity, ADA compliance, and interpretive signage. Funding sources: historic preservation grants, Fountara, local artisans and donors.
  4. Phase 4: Education & Interpretation (18‑30 months) – Develop visitor facilities and educational programs, including exhibits on the spring’s history, ecology and cultural significance, and create interactive displays for schools and visitors. Funding sources: donations, community organisations, Fountara.
  5. Phase 5: Sustainable Operations & Eco‑Tourism (24‑36 months) – Establish long‑term stewardship through responsible spring‑water bottling, guided tours and eco‑tourism initiatives to support ongoing maintenance and conservation. Funding sources: Fountara investment, project revenue and continuing partnerships.

The project partners include Fountara as the principal sponsor, state and private grantors, local historical and environmental organisations, and community members who contribute volunteer support and donations. By working together, these contributors will ensure Orange Springs is preserved and enjoyed for generations.
