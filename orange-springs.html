Project Overview

The Orange Springs Restoration & Preservation Project aims to restore and enhance the natural springs and wetlands in the Orange Springs area of Florida. Our goal is to protect water quality, restore native vegetation, and provide educational and recreational opportunities. Restoration efforts are guided by research, local knowledge, and collaboration with agencies and stakeholders.

Restoration Phases

Phase 1 – Archaeological and Burial‑Site Survey

Conduct a state‑licensed Phase IA/IB survey covering Seminole Spring, Orange Springs and the Orange Creek corridor.

Identify and protect any cultural or burial sites per Florida Statute §872.05 and coordinate with tribal representatives.

Phase 2 – Creek and Spring Restoration (Multi‑County Corridor)

Remove muck, debris and invasives from Seminole Spring and Orange Springs downstream to Rodman Reservoir, Orange Lake and Lochloosa Lake.

Restore hydrology and improve oxygen levels while minimizing mechanical disturbance.

Phase 3 – Vegetation and Shoreline Stabilization

Replant native wetland and upland species.

Employ bio‑engineering techniques such as coir logs, root matting, live staking and native sod stabilization to secure shorelines.

Phase 4 – Historic and Cultural Preservation

Stabilize and restore the historic James W. Townsend House using the Secretary of the Interior’s Standards.

Produce as‑built documentation and interpretive materials to educate the public about the site’s cultural significance.

Phase 5 – Conservation Buffer and Monitoring Network

Establish a vegetated buffer linking Seminole Spring, Orange Springs and Orange Creek.

Implement quarterly monitoring of vegetation, erosion and water quality, with annual DEP and DHR reporting to ensure long‑term project success.

Long‑Term Development Goals

To ensure the Orange Springs Restoration & Preservation Project remains resilient and self‑sustaining, we are planning several infrastructure projects that provide community benefits and emergency preparedness.

Dome 1 – Water Bagging & Storage

Construct a climate‑controlled geodesic dome (“Dome 1”) equipped with an industrial water bagging line. This facility will produce sealed potable‑water bags for emergency use and to supply Fountara’s Water Bank. Bagging operations ensure that safe drinking water is available during hurricanes, floods and other disasters.

Surplus bagged water will be stored on‑site, creating a reserve that can be deployed quickly when needed, and integrated with the Water Bank storage program.

Free Community Storm & Disaster Shelter

Develop a free‑to‑public storm and disaster shelter integrated into the restoration campus. The shelter will provide secure space, emergency supplies and communication resources for local residents during severe weather events.

Powered by renewable energy and supplied with water from Dome 1, the shelter will serve as a community anchor when critical services are disrupted, ensuring Orange Springs remains prepared for storms, floods and other emergencies.
